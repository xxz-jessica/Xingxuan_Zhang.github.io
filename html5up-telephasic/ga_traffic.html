Website Traffic Overview

Overview of website traffic, traffic sources, and the industry benchmark (Club&Organization) of the COPA website since April 2nd to April 18th.

The COPA website migrated to the new system on April 2nd. On the migration day, there were 1,674 uses visited the new website. On the next day, the number of users increased to 3,103, about 85.36% higher than the previous day. However, after that, the number of users decreased to about 2,000 users. On April 11th, there were only 1,425 users visited the website.

Although the number of users decreased, the average session duration increased since April 3rd, indicating that users spent more time on the website.

As for the page views per session, on April 2nd and 3rd, the page views per session was low but increased later, demonstrating that users did not read many pages/posts at the beginning but became more and more comfortable about digesting pages on the new website. The bounce rate also decreased from 34.78% to 16.07% since the migration, which supports the conclusion.

Based on data from April 2nd to 19th, users, on average, generated about 4 sessions on the website and viewed about 10.54 pages per session. For each session, users spent about 24 minutes, and 19.59% of users visited the website and quit immediately without doing anything.

User Retension

This is the cohort analysis and shows the retention rate of each cohort. Users who visited the website on April 2nd tend to have a better retention rate in the following 12 days.

Industry Benchmark (Club & Organization)

When setting the benchmark as the clubs and organization, COPA's website performed pretty well except COPA does not attract many users. It makes sense due to the high entry barry of owning an aircraft. To be noticed, the new users would decrease further while more and more existed members visit the website and become returning users.
